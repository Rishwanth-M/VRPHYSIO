Dry Needling Therapy

Trigger point needling to reduce pain and spasm—integrated with manual therapy and progressive exercise.

About This Service

We use fine, sterile needles to deactivate myofascial trigger points and reduce protective muscle guarding. Treatment is paired with mobility and strength work to lock in range-of-motion gains and improve function.

Common Conditions We Treat

  • Neck & shoulder tension
  • Headache trigger points
  • Low-back spasm
  • Glute & hip tightness
  • Calf & hamstring knots
  • Forearm pain
  • Postural overuse pain
  • Myofascial pain syndromes
  • Athletic tightness

Our Clinical Approach

1. Assessment & Diagnosis

Detailed history, movement analysis, special tests, and functional screening to identify drivers of pain and dysfunction.

2. Manual Therapy

Joint and soft-tissue techniques plus neural mobilisation to restore motion and reduce sensitivity.

3. Therapeutic Exercise

Progressive strength, control, and flexibility work to build durable capacity.

4. Adjunct Modalities

TENS, IFT, ultrasound, and cryotherapy as needed to support recovery.

What to Expect During Recovery

Early Stage

Pain and swelling control; gentle range of motion and breathing work.

Strength Phase

Load progression, balance, and endurance with emphasis on quality of movement.

Return & Maintenance

Task or sport-specific progressions plus ergonomic and lifestyle strategies.

Detailed Case Study

Patient Summary

Age/Gender: 35M
Status: 6 weeks post ACL reconstruction
Main Concerns: Knee stiffness, instability, quadriceps weakness
Goal: Return to sport drills by 5 months

Rehabilitation Protocol
Phase 1 (Weeks 6–8): Pain & Mobility
  • Cryotherapy and NMES for quadriceps activation
  • Patellofemoral mobilisations (Grade I–II)
  • Heel slides to 110°, straight leg raises, terminal knee extensions
Phase 2 (Weeks 8–12): Strength & Proprioception
  • Mini-squats, wall slides, step-ups
  • Single-leg balance and wobble board training
  • Progressive resistance and gait re-education
Phase 3 (Weeks 12–20): Functional Reintegration
  • Jogging return protocol and cadence control
  • Agility ladder, lateral hops, deceleration drills
  • Hip–knee alignment and landing technique training
Outcome & Follow-Up
  • Knee ROM restored to 0–135°
  • Quadriceps strength 5/5; hop test >95% symmetry
  • Pain-free ADLs and return to sport drills
Therapist Commentary

Early NMES and progressive closed-chain work prevented inhibition and supported strength symmetry. The plan aligned with evidence-informed orthopedic rehab standards and the patient adhered closely to post-op protocols.
