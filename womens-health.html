Detailed Case Study

Patient Summary

Age/Gender: 35M
Condition: 6 wks post ACL reconstruction
Complaint: Knee stiffness, instability, quadriceps weakness
Goal: Return to sport drills by 5 months

Rehabilitation Protocol
Phase 1 (Weeks 6–8): Pain & Mobility
  • Cryotherapy and NMES for quadriceps activation
  • Patello-femoral mobilisations (Grade I–II)
  • Heel slides up to 110°, Straight Leg Raises (SLR), Terminal Knee Extensions (TKE)
Phase 2 (Weeks 8–12): Strength & Proprioception
  • Mini-squats, wall slides, step-ups
  • Single-leg balance, wobble board training
  • Progressive resistance exercises; gait re-education
Phase 3 (Weeks 12–20): Functional Reintegration
  • Jogging return protocol and cadence control
  • Agility ladder, lateral hops, deceleration drills
  • Hip–knee alignment and landing technique training
Outcome & Follow-Up
  • Knee ROM restored to 0–135°
  • Quadriceps strength 5/5; hop test >95% symmetry
  • Pain-free ADLs and return to sport drills
Therapist Commentary

Early NMES and closed-chain progressions helped prevent inhibition and promote strength symmetry. The patient followed all post-op protocols closely, aligning with evidence-based orthopedic rehab standards.
